Discovered Correlations

Poverty: There are clear correlations between poverty and all three y-axis datasets. Obesity, smoking, and a lack of health insurance all increase as the rate of poverty increases.

Age: As median age increases, so does the rate of smoking. The inverse relationship exists between age and having no health insurance.

Household Income: Levels of obesity, smoking, and being uninsured decrease as income increases.
